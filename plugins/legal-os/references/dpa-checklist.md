# DPA Compliance Checklist — GDPR Article 28

## Mandatory Elements

Every Data Processing Agreement must contain ALL of the following elements per Article 28(3). Check each item and note the quality of the provision.

### Checklist

| # | Requirement | Art. 28(3) | Present? | Quality | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Subject matter of processing** | (a) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Description of what processing occurs |
| 2 | **Duration of processing** | (a) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Linked to contract term or specified period |
| 3 | **Nature and purpose of processing** | (a) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Why is data being processed |
| 4 | **Types of personal data** | (a) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Specific categories listed |
| 5 | **Categories of data subjects** | (a) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Who the data relates to |
| 6 | **Processing only on documented instructions** | (a) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Must include obligation to inform if instruction infringes GDPR |
| 7 | **Confidentiality of processing personnel** | (b) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Statutory obligation or contractual commitment |
| 8 | **Security measures (Art. 32)** | (c) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Specific measures or reference to security annex |
| 9 | **Sub-processor conditions** | (d) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Prior specific or general authorization |
| 10 | **Assistance with data subject rights** | (e) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Technical and organizational measures to assist |
| 11 | **Assistance with Art. 32-36 obligations** | (f) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation |
| 12 | **Deletion or return at end of contract** | (g) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Controller's choice; delete all copies |
| 13 | **Audit and inspection rights** | (h) | YES/NO | GOOD/ADEQUATE/WEAK | Make available all information necessary; allow audits |

---

## Quality Assessment Criteria

### Element Quality: GOOD
- Specific, detailed provisions
- Clear obligations and timeframes
- Aligned with org's standards
- No unusual limitations or carve-outs

### Element Quality: ADEQUATE
- Provisions present and functional
- Some areas could be strengthened
- Acceptable for moderate-risk processing

### Element Quality: WEAK
- Provisions present but vague or limited
- Significant carve-outs or limitations
- Needs improvement for higher-risk processing

## Key Negotiation Points

### 1. Sub-Processor Approval (Element 9)

| Position | Strength |
|---|---|
| Prior specific written authorization for each sub-processor | Strongest — full control |
| Prior general authorization with notification and right to object (30+ days) | Acceptable — balanced |
| General authorization with short objection period (<14 days) | Weak — insufficient time to assess |
| No approval mechanism | Non-compliant — reject |

**Check:**
- Is a current sub-processor list provided?
- What's the notification period for new sub-processors?
- What happens if the controller objects?
- Are back-to-back DPA obligations imposed on sub-processors?

### 2. Breach Notification (Element 11)

| Timeframe | Assessment |
|---|---|
| ≤24 hours | Best practice |
| ≤48 hours | Good — gives controller time within 72h window |
| ≤72 hours | Marginal — no buffer for controller's own assessment |
| >72 hours or "without undue delay" only | Unacceptable — could cause controller to miss Art. 33 deadline |

**Check:**
- What information is provided in the initial notification?
- Is there a defined point of contact for breach reporting?
- Is assistance with Art. 33/34 notifications included?

### 3. Audit Rights (Element 13)

| Provision | Assessment |
|---|---|
| On-site audit with reasonable notice (30 days) | Strongest |
| Annual third-party audit report (SOC 2/ISO) + on-site for cause | Acceptable |
| Audit report provided annually, no on-site | Adequate for low-risk |
| "Desk audit" only or questionnaire-based | Weak |
| No audit rights | Non-compliant — reject |

**Check:**
- Who bears the cost of audits?
- How often can audits be conducted?
- What access is provided (systems, facilities, records)?
- Is there a confidentiality obligation for audit findings?

### 4. Data Location and Transfers (related to Elements 1-5)

**Check:**
- Where is data stored and processed?
- Are processing locations specified in the DPA or an annex?
- If outside EEA: what transfer mechanism is used?
- Can the processor change locations without notification?

### 5. End-of-Contract Data Handling (Element 12)

**Check:**
- Does the controller have a choice between deletion and return?
- What's the timeframe for deletion/return?
- Does the processor certify deletion?
- Are all copies (including backups) covered?
- Is there a retention period for legal obligations?

## Red Flags in DPAs

| Red Flag | Risk Level | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Missing 3+ mandatory elements | CRITICAL | Reject until remediated |
| No sub-processor visibility | HIGH | Negotiate or reject |
| Breach notification >72h | HIGH | Negotiate down to ≤48h |
| No audit rights at all | HIGH | Negotiate or reject |
| Data processing "worldwide" with no safeguards | HIGH | Negotiate location restrictions |
| Processor can change terms unilaterally | HIGH | Remove or reject |
| Controller bears all costs of processor's obligations | MEDIUM | Negotiate fair allocation |
| Vague security measures ("industry standard") | MEDIUM | Request specifics or certification |
| No assistance with data subject rights | MEDIUM | Add provision |
| Deletion timeframe >90 days | MEDIUM | Negotiate shorter period |
