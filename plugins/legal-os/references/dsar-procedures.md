# DSAR Handling Procedures

## Data Subject Access Request Workflow

### Step 1: Receive and Log

When a DSAR is received:

1. **Log the request immediately** — record:
   - Date received
   - Channel received (email, letter, phone, in-person, web form)
   - Data subject's name and contact details
   - Right(s) being exercised
   - Any specific data or processing activities referenced
   - Request ID (assign a unique identifier)

2. **Start the clock** — 1 calendar month from receipt (Art. 12(3))
   - If received on March 15 → deadline is April 15
   - If month has no equivalent date (e.g., Jan 31 → Feb 28/29)
   - Can be extended by 2 months for complex/numerous requests (must notify within 1 month)

### Step 2: Validate the Request

**Identity verification:**
- Can you reasonably confirm the requester is the data subject?
- If uncertain: request additional proof (don't use this to delay unreasonably)
- For online accounts: match with account authentication
- For other requests: photo ID, matching known contact details
- Clock pauses while waiting for verification

**Scope determination:**
Which right(s) are being exercised?

| Right | Article | Response Obligation |
|---|---|---|
| Access | Art. 15 | Provide copy of data + processing details |
| Rectification | Art. 16 | Correct inaccurate data |
| Erasure | Art. 17 | Delete data (with exceptions) |
| Restriction | Art. 18 | Mark and restrict processing |
| Portability | Art. 20 | Provide in structured, machine-readable format |
| Objection | Art. 21 | Stop processing unless compelling grounds |
| Automated decisions | Art. 22 | Meaningful information about logic + consequences |

### Step 3: Search and Collect Data

**Data sources to check:**
- Customer/user databases
- Email systems (correspondence with the individual)
- CRM systems
- HR systems (if employee)
- Marketing databases
- Analytics systems (if identifiable)
- Backup systems (if readily accessible)
- Paper records
- Third-party processors (instruct them to search)

**What to provide (Art. 15):**
- Purposes of processing
- Categories of personal data
- Recipients or categories of recipients
- Retention periods (or criteria for determining)
- Existence of data subject rights
- Right to lodge complaint with supervisory authority
- Source of data (if not collected directly)
- Automated decision-making info (logic, significance, consequences)
- International transfer safeguards

### Step 4: Apply Exemptions

**Legitimate exemptions to consider:**

| Exemption | When It Applies |
|---|---|
| **Rights of others** | Disclosing would adversely affect rights/freedoms of others (redact third-party data) |
| **Legal privilege** | Data covered by legal professional privilege |
| **Manifestly excessive** | Repetitive requests by the same person (can charge fee or refuse) |
| **Legal claims** | Data needed for establishing, exercising, or defending legal claims |
| **Archiving/research** | Processing for archiving, scientific/historical research, or statistical purposes |
| **National security** | Member State exemptions for national security, defense, public security |

**Important:** Exemptions must be applied on a case-by-case basis and documented. Refusing a request entirely is rare — partial disclosure with redactions is more common.

### Step 5: Prepare Response

**Response must include:**
1. Confirmation of whether personal data is being processed
2. If yes: a copy of the data (first copy free, subsequent copies at reasonable cost)
3. All information listed in Art. 15(1)-(2)
4. If data was not found: confirm no personal data is held
5. If exemptions applied: explain which exemption and why

**Format:**
- Provide in commonly used electronic format (if request was electronic)
- Secure transmission method (encrypted email, secure portal)
- Do not send unencrypted personal data by regular email

**Response templates:**

#### Acknowledgment (send within 3 business days):
> Dear [Name],
>
> We acknowledge receipt of your data subject access request dated [date]. We are processing your request and will respond within one calendar month (by [deadline]).
>
> [If identity verification needed:] To verify your identity, please provide [specific documentation needed].
>
> Reference: [Request ID]

#### Full response:
> Dear [Name],
>
> Further to your request dated [date] (ref: [ID]), we can confirm that we do/do not process your personal data.
>
> [If data processed:] Please find enclosed a copy of your personal data along with the following information about our processing:
> - Purposes: [list]
> - Categories: [list]
> - Recipients: [list]
> - Retention: [periods]
> - Your rights: [list]
>
> [If exemptions applied:] We have redacted [description] under [exemption] because [rationale].
>
> If you have questions or wish to exercise further rights, please contact [DPO email].

#### Extension notification (if needed, send within 1 month):
> Dear [Name],
>
> We are writing regarding your request dated [date] (ref: [ID]). Due to the complexity/volume of your request, we require an additional [1-2] month(s) to respond fully, as permitted under GDPR Article 12(3). We now expect to respond by [new deadline].
>
> [Explain the complexity briefly]

### Step 6: Deliver and Close

1. **Send the response** via secure method
2. **Log completion** — date sent, method, what was provided/withheld
3. **Set review date** — if erasure was requested, verify deletion across all systems
4. **Archive the request file** — retain for evidence of compliance (recommend 3 years)

### Deadline Summary

| Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge receipt | 3 business days (best practice) |
| Respond to request | 1 calendar month from receipt |
| Extension notification | Within the initial 1 month |
| Maximum response time | 3 calendar months from receipt |
| Identity verification pause | Clock stops until verification received |
