# Legal Memo Templates

## Standard Legal Memo (IRAC Format)

```markdown
# Legal Memorandum
**To:** {recipient}
**From:** Legal OS — Legal Researcher
**Date:** {date}
**Re:** {subject}
**Confidential:** Yes

---

## I. Issue

{State the legal question(s) precisely. Frame as a question:
"Whether [action/situation] complies with [regulation/requirement] given [relevant facts]."}

## II. Short Answer

{1-2 sentence direct answer to the question. Include the key conclusion and primary basis.}

## III. Statement of Facts

{Relevant facts that form the basis of the analysis. Include:
- Organization details (name, jurisdiction, industry)
- Specific situation or proposed action
- Relevant data processing activities
- Current compliance measures in place
- Any time constraints or deadlines}

## IV. Applicable Law

### Primary Legislation
{Cite the specific regulation, directive, or law with article references}

### Regulatory Guidance
{Cite relevant EDPB guidelines, DPA guidance, or Commission guidance}

### Case Law / Precedent
{Cite relevant CJEU rulings, national court decisions, or DPA enforcement actions}

## V. Analysis

{Apply the law to the facts. Structure by issue if multiple sub-questions.

For each issue:
1. State the legal rule
2. Apply it to the org's specific facts
3. Consider counterarguments or exceptions
4. Address jurisdictional variations if relevant
5. Assess the risk level}

## VI. Conclusion and Recommendations

### Risk Assessment
**Overall Risk Level:** {LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / CRITICAL}

### Recommendations
1. {Primary recommendation}
2. {Secondary recommendation}
3. {Alternative approach if primary is not feasible}

### Action Items
| # | Action | Priority | Deadline |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | {action} | {HIGH/MED/LOW} | {date} |

### Caveats
- {Note jurisdictional limitations of the analysis}
- {Note any areas where the law is unsettled}
- {Recommend consulting local counsel where needed}

---
*This memorandum is AI-assisted legal research for informational purposes only. It does not constitute legal advice. Consult qualified legal counsel for formal opinions.*
```
