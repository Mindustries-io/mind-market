# GDPR Key Articles Reference

Quick reference for the most commonly cited GDPR articles. For full text, search EUR-Lex.

## Chapter II — Principles (Art. 5-11)

| Article | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Art. 5** | Principles of processing | Lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity/confidentiality, accountability |
| **Art. 6** | Lawful basis | Six bases: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest, legitimate interests |
| **Art. 7** | Conditions for consent | Freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous; withdrawal as easy as giving; records required |
| **Art. 8** | Child's consent | Under 16 (or lower, min 13, per Member State) requires parental consent for ISS |
| **Art. 9** | Special categories | Racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union, genetics, biometrics, health, sex life/orientation — prohibited unless Art. 9(2) exception applies |
| **Art. 10** | Criminal data | Processing only under official authority control or authorized by law |
| **Art. 11** | Processing not requiring identification | No obligation to maintain additional data solely for identification of data subjects |

## Chapter III — Data Subject Rights (Art. 12-23)

| Article | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Art. 12** | Transparent communication | Clear, plain language; respond within 1 month (extendable by 2) |
| **Art. 13** | Information at collection | Identity, purposes, lawful basis, recipients, transfers, retention, rights, automated decisions |
| **Art. 14** | Information not collected from subject | Same as Art. 13 + source of data; provide within 1 month |
| **Art. 15** | Right of access | Copy of personal data + processing info; first copy free |
| **Art. 16** | Right to rectification | Correct inaccurate data without undue delay |
| **Art. 17** | Right to erasure | "Right to be forgotten" — erase when no longer necessary, consent withdrawn, objection, unlawful |
| **Art. 18** | Right to restriction | Restrict processing when accuracy contested, unlawful, or objection pending |
| **Art. 19** | Notification obligation | Notify recipients of rectification, erasure, or restriction |
| **Art. 20** | Data portability | Provide data in structured, machine-readable format; transmit to another controller |
| **Art. 21** | Right to object | Object to processing based on Art. 6(1)(e)/(f); absolute right for direct marketing |
| **Art. 22** | Automated decision-making | Right not to be subject to solely automated decisions with legal/significant effects; exceptions apply |

## Chapter IV — Controller & Processor (Art. 24-43)

| Article | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Art. 24** | Controller responsibility | Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures; demonstrate compliance |
| **Art. 25** | Privacy by design/default | Data protection by design and by default at all stages |
| **Art. 26** | Joint controllers | Transparent arrangement determining responsibilities; available to data subjects |
| **Art. 27** | Representatives | Controllers/processors outside EU must designate EU representative |
| **Art. 28** | Processor | Written contract with specific terms; sub-processor approval; audit rights |
| **Art. 29** | Processing under authority | Processor acts only on controller instructions |
| **Art. 30** | Records of processing (RoPA) | Maintain records; exemption for <250 employees unless high-risk processing |
| **Art. 31** | Cooperation with authority | Cooperate with supervisory authority on request |
| **Art. 32** | Security of processing | Appropriate technical/organizational measures (pseudonymization, encryption, resilience, testing) |
| **Art. 33** | Breach notification to authority | Without undue delay, not later than **72 hours** after awareness; unless unlikely to result in risk |
| **Art. 34** | Breach notification to data subject | Without undue delay when **high risk** to rights and freedoms |
| **Art. 35** | DPIA | Required when processing likely results in **high risk**; systematic profiling, large-scale special categories, systematic monitoring |
| **Art. 36** | Prior consultation | Consult supervisory authority when DPIA indicates high residual risk |
| **Art. 37** | DPO designation | Required for: public authorities, core activities involving large-scale monitoring, large-scale special categories |
| **Art. 38** | DPO position | Independent, no conflicts of interest, adequate resources, reports to highest management |
| **Art. 39** | DPO tasks | Inform, advise, monitor compliance, cooperate with authority, act as contact point |

## Chapter V — Transfers (Art. 44-50)

| Article | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Art. 44** | General principle | Transfers only if Chapter V conditions met |
| **Art. 45** | Adequacy decision | Commission determines adequate level of protection (current: UK, Japan, South Korea, etc.) |
| **Art. 46** | Appropriate safeguards | SCCs, BCRs, codes of conduct, certification mechanisms |
| **Art. 47** | Binding Corporate Rules | Group-wide internal rules approved by lead supervisory authority |
| **Art. 49** | Derogations | Explicit consent, contractual necessity, public interest, vital interests, public register |

## Chapter VI-VII — Authorities & Cooperation (Art. 51-76)

Key provisions: independence of supervisory authorities, one-stop-shop mechanism, consistency mechanism, mutual assistance, EDPB role.

## Chapter VIII — Remedies & Penalties (Art. 77-84)

| Article | Title | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Art. 77** | Right to lodge complaint | Data subject can complain to supervisory authority |
| **Art. 79** | Right to judicial remedy | Against controller or processor |
| **Art. 82** | Right to compensation | Material and non-material damage; joint and several liability |
| **Art. 83** | Administrative fines | Tier 1: up to €10M / 2% global turnover; Tier 2: up to **€20M / 4%** global turnover |
| **Art. 84** | Other penalties | Member States may lay down other penalties |

## Fine Tiers

### Tier 1 — Up to €10M or 2% global turnover
- Art. 8 (child's consent), Art. 11 (identification), Art. 25-39 (controller/processor obligations), Art. 42-43 (certification)

### Tier 2 — Up to €20M or 4% global turnover
- Art. 5-7 (principles, lawful basis, consent), Art. 9 (special categories), Art. 12-22 (data subject rights), Art. 44-49 (transfers)
